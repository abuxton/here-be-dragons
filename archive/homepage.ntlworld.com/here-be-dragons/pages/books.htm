Useful Books

 

These are some of the books I have found useful for research and costume making for LRP. As costume books tend to be rather expensive I found most of these in either the public libraries (most will get you anything you order if it is obtainable) or at second-hand bookshops.
  • Period Costume for Stage and Screen: patterns for women's dress
    Volume 1 1500-1800 (ISBN 0-88 734-610-3) Players Press
    Volume 2 1800-1909 (ISBN 0-88734-609-X) Players Press
    Medieval-1500 (ISBN 0-88734-653-7) Players Press
    Jean Hunniset
    These include informative text, patterns and detailed instruction on how to make the costumes, from the corset up. Very good "big frocks", and the Tudor/Elizabethan overrobes are good for anything. I think these are about £35.00 each, but well worth getting from the library. Good general construction details as well. The medieval book includes italian renaissance dresses to die for!
  • Patterns of Fashion 3(1560-1620)
    Arnold, J (ISBN 0333 382845) Macmillan
    Drawings of men's and women's clothing showing their construction and detailed patterns. Not for the beginner to construct, but good for inspiration and to copy some elements.
  • The Evolution of Fashion 1066-1930
    H Hamilton-Hill & P Bucknell (ISBN 0 7134 5818 6) Batsford
    Line drawings, description and patterns for men and women's dress. Good for LRP as this book covers the norman and medieaval periods.
  • English Costume of the Early Middle Ages
    English Costume of the Later Middle Ages
    English Costume in the Age of Elizabeth
    Iris Brooke
    The above are all part of a series covering English costume. They include black and white drawings, with some in colour, of costume in chronological order, with a description. They may not be that correct historically (according to some of the comments in the historical costuming FAQ), but provide useful LRP inspiration.
  • Costume 1066-1966
    Peacock, John (ISBN 0-5000-27404-5)Thames & Hudson
    Detailed line drawings of men and women's costume. I think there is now a new edition, taking the period covered up to the 1990s.
